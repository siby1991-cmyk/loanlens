SBI Home Loan — Detailed Review

State Bank of India

Best for: Government employees, first-time buyers, large loan amounts

  • Lowest starting rate: 8.40% p.a.
  • Special rates for women borrowers (extra 0.05% discount)
  • SBI Maxgain — overdraft facility on home loan
  • No prepayment penalty on floating rate loans
  • Available at 22,000+ branches across India

SBI Pros

  • Lowest interest rates in the market consistently
  • Lowest processing fee (0.35%, capped at ₹10,000)
  • Trusted government bank — high security
  • Special schemes for defence personnel, teachers, and senior citizens

SBI Cons

  • Slower processing — can take 3–4 weeks for approval
  • More paperwork compared to private banks
  • Branch visits may be required multiple times
  • Customer service can be inconsistent

HDFC Bank Home Loan — Detailed Review

HDFC Bank

Best for: Salaried professionals, self-employed, fast processing needed

  • Competitive rate: 8.50% p.a. onwards
  • Approval in 48 hours for salaried customers
  • Fully digital application process
  • HDFC Reach — loans for self-employed in smaller cities
  • Top-up loan facility easily available

HDFC Pros

  • Fastest processing — often approved within 48 hours
  • Excellent digital experience — minimal branch visits
  • Flexible repayment options including step-up EMI
  • Better customer service and relationship manager

HDFC Cons

  • Slightly higher interest rate than SBI
  • Higher processing fee (0.5%)
  • Stricter eligibility for self-employed borrowers

Who Should Choose SBI?

  • Government employees (special rate discounts available)
  • Borrowers who want the absolute lowest interest rate
  • Those taking large loans (₹75 lakh+)
  • First-time home buyers looking for the most economical option
  • Customers who already bank with SBI

Who Should Choose HDFC?

  • Salaried professionals who need fast approval
  • Self-employed individuals with good income documentation
  • Those who prefer a digital, hassle-free process
  • Borrowers who may need a top-up loan later
  • People in metros and tier-1 cities

Our Verdict

For pure savings — choose SBI. The lower rate and processing fee save you more money over the long term.

For convenience and speed — choose HDFC. The digital process and fast approval make it ideal for busy professionals.

Pro tip: Get pre-approval from both banks, compare the final offers including all fees, then decide.
