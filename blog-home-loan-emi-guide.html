Buying a home is one of the biggest financial decisions of your life. Before you sign any loan agreement, you need to know exactly how much you'll pay every month — and over the entire loan tenure. This guide explains everything about home loan EMI calculation in India for 2026.

What is Home Loan EMI?

EMI stands for Equated Monthly Instalment. It is the fixed amount you pay to your bank every month until your home loan is fully repaid. Each EMI payment has two components:

  • Principal component — the portion that reduces your loan balance
  • Interest component — the cost charged by the bank for lending money

In the early months of your loan, most of your EMI goes toward interest. As the loan matures, more of each payment goes toward the principal. This is called an amortizing loan.

Key Insight: On a ₹50 lakh home loan at 8.5% for 20 years, you'll pay approximately ₹43.4 lakhs in interest alone — nearly as much as the loan itself. Understanding this helps you make smarter decisions.

EMI Calculation Formula

The formula used by all banks in India to calculate home loan EMI is:

EMI = P × r × (1 + r)^n / [(1 + r)^n - 1] Where: P = Principal loan amount r = Monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100) n = Total number of monthly instalments (years × 12)

Step-by-Step Example

Scenario: ₹50 Lakh Home Loan

  • Loan Amount: ₹50,00,000
  • Interest Rate: 8.5% per annum
  • Tenure: 20 years (240 months)

Monthly rate (r) = 8.5 ÷ 12 ÷ 100 = 0.007083

EMI = 50,00,000 × 0.007083 × (1.007083)^240 ÷ [(1.007083)^240 - 1]

Result: EMI = ₹43,391 per month

Over 20 years, you'll pay a total of ₹1,04,13,840 — meaning ₹54,13,840 in interest on a ₹50 lakh loan.

Important: A longer tenure means lower EMI but significantly more total interest paid. A 20-year loan pays roughly 2x more interest than a 10-year loan on the same amount.

Factors That Affect Your Home Loan EMI

1. Loan Amount

Higher the loan amount, higher the EMI. Most banks offer home loans up to 80–90% of the property value. The remaining amount is your down payment.

2. Interest Rate

Even a 0.5% difference in rate can save lakhs over a 20-year tenure. Always compare rates before choosing a bank. Use our bank comparison tool to see the difference instantly.

3. Loan Tenure

Longer tenure = lower monthly EMI but higher total interest. Shorter tenure = higher EMI but significant interest savings. Choose a tenure where the EMI is 35–40% of your monthly take-home salary.

4. Your Credit Score

A CIBIL score above 750 qualifies you for the best interest rates. Scores below 650 may lead to rejection or higher rates. Check your score for free on Paisabazaar before applying.

Tips to Reduce Your Home Loan EMI

  1. Make a larger down payment — Paying 25–30% upfront reduces the loan amount and EMI significantly
  2. Choose a longer tenure initially — Start with 20–25 years for lower EMI, then prepay when you have surplus income
  3. Negotiate the interest rate — Banks have flexibility especially for existing customers with good credit history
  4. Do a balance transfer — If another bank offers 0.5% lower rate, transferring your loan can save lakhs
  5. Make annual prepayments — Even one extra EMI per year can reduce your tenure by 3–4 years

Prepayment tip: Paying ₹2 lakh extra in year 2 of a ₹50 lakh loan at 8.5% can save over ₹6 lakh in interest and reduce tenure by 2.5 years. Use our prepayment calculator to see your exact savings.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the maximum home loan I can get?

Banks typically offer home loans up to 80% of the property value (for loans above ₹75 lakhs) or up to 90% for smaller loans. Your eligibility also depends on income — most banks allow EMI up to 40–50% of monthly income.

Can I get a home loan with a salary of ₹30,000 per month?

Yes. With ₹30,000 monthly salary, your maximum EMI should be around ₹12,000–₹15,000. At 8.5% for 20 years, this qualifies you for approximately ₹15–₹17 lakh loan. A joint loan with a spouse can increase eligibility significantly.

Is it better to take a shorter or longer tenure?

If your monthly cash flow allows, a shorter tenure (10–15 years) saves significantly more in interest. However, a 20–25 year tenure gives you lower EMI and more financial flexibility. You can always prepay to reduce the effective tenure.

What documents are needed for a home loan?

Typically: Identity proof (Aadhaar/PAN), Address proof, Last 3 months salary slips, Last 6 months bank statements, Form 16, Property documents and agreement to sale.

How does RBI repo rate affect my home loan EMI?

Most home loans are on floating rates linked to MCLR or repo rate. When RBI increases the repo rate, your interest rate and EMI increase. When RBI cuts rates, your EMI reduces or tenure shortens. As of March 2026, the repo rate is 6.25%.
